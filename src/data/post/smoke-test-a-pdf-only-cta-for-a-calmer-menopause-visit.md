### A calmer way to prepare for the visit

When symptoms change during perimenopause or menopause, the hardest part is often not deciding whether care matters. It is knowing what to bring into the conversation so the visit starts clearly instead of in a rush. This smoke-test article keeps the page intentionally simple: text, one practical download, and no image payload fields.

### What the checklist helps organize

The downloadable checklist gives a patient a concise place to capture what changed, when it started, what makes it better or worse, and which questions should not get lost during the appointment. It is not a diagnostic tool. It is a preparation aid for a more focused clinical conversation.

### Why this test is intentionally plain

This page is designed to isolate the publish path. There is no featured image, no inline image, no image media entry, and no image artifact reference anywhere in the reader-facing article body. The only non-text artifact attached to the reader journey is the generated PDF checklist behind the button below.
